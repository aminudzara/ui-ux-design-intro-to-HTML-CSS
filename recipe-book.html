Masa - Northern Nigerian Rice Cakes

Prep Time
20 min
Fermentation
12-16 hours
Cook Time
15-20 min
Servings
12-15

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Tuwo Rice (or short-grain rice) 2-3 cups, soaked 6 hours or overnight
Cooked White Rice 1 cup
Active Dry Yeast 1-1.5 tablespoons
Sugar 2-4 tablespoons
Salt ½ teaspoon
Baking Powder 1 teaspoon
Powdered Milk (optional) ½ cup
Warm Water ½-¾ cup, as needed
Oil for frying As needed

Instructions

  1. Soak the Rice: Wash the soaked rice a couple of times until water runs clear. Drain well and set aside.
  2. Proof the Yeast: In a small bowl, mix the active dry yeast, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and ¼ cup of warm water. Let sit for about 10 minutes until frothy.
  3. Blend the Batter: Transfer the drained soaked rice to a powerful blender. Add the cooked rice and ½ cup of warm water. Blend until smooth and creamy like custard or pap.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Pour the blended rice mixture into a large bowl. Add the frothy yeast mixture, remaining sugar (to taste), salt, baking powder, and powdered milk if using. Mix well with a wooden spoon.
  5. Ferment: Cover the mixture with a clean cloth and let it rest for 12-16 hours. The batter will rise and become airy with tiny holes throughout.
  6. Prepare for Frying: The batter is now ready to fry. Heat oil in a special masa pan or non-stick frying pan over medium heat. The oil should be just warm, not hot.
  7. Fry the Masa: Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil (about 2-3 minutes per side) until golden brown on both sides. The masa should puff up and become light and airy inside.
  8. Drain and Serve: Remove from oil and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Serve warm with yogurt, fruit purees, kuli-kuli, or your favorite sauce.

Okra Soup - Classic Nigerian Delicacy

Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
45-60 min
Servings
6-8
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Fresh Okra, chopped 4-5 cups (about 25 pieces)
Beef or Goat Meat 1 pound, cubed
Smoked Turkey/Catfish ½ pound
Shrimp (fresh or dried) ½ cup
Red Palm Oil ½ cup
Ground Crayfish 2 tablespoons
Locust Beans (Iru) 2 tablespoons
Red Bell Pepper, blended 2 medium
Habanero Pepper, blended 1-2 (adjust to taste)
Onion, chopped 1 medium
Bouillon/Maggi Cubes 2-3 cubes
Salt & Black Pepper To taste
Water As needed

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Stock: Place beef or goat meat in a large pot with chopped onions, Maggi cubes, salt, and water. Boil until tender (about 35-45 minutes). Reserve both the meat and the flavorful broth.
  2. Prepare the Okra: Wash fresh okra and slice it into manageable pieces. You can blend half of it to get a slimy texture and chop the other half for texture. Set aside.
  3. Heat the Palm Oil: In a pot over medium heat, warm the red palm oil for 5-8 minutes. This adds depth to the soup.
  4. Add Aromatics: Add the blended red and habanero peppers to the warm palm oil. Stir and cook for about 3-5 minutes to develop flavors.
  5. Add Seasonings: Add the ground crayfish and locust beans, stirring well. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Pour in Stock: Add the reserved meat stock and bring to a boil. If you don't have enough stock, add water.
  7. Add Okra: Add the blended okra first, stirring vigorously to combine. Then add the chopped okra pieces. Stir well.
  8. Add Proteins: Return the cooked meat to the pot. Add the smoked fish, shrimp, and any remaining proteins. Stir to combine.
  9. Simmer: Let the soup simmer on medium heat for 5-10 minutes. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed. The soup should be thick and slimy but still have good flow.
  10. Serve: Serve hot with your favorite swallow (pounded yam, eba, amala) or over rice. Enjoy!

Baobab Soup (Miyan Kuka) - Northern Treasure

Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
40-50 min
Servings
6
Region
Northern Nigeria

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Kuka Powder (Ground Dried Baobab Leaves) 1 cup
Beef or Goat Meat 1 pound, cubed
Dried Fish or Smoked Catfish ½ pound
Dried Crayfish 2-3 tablespoons, ground
Locust Beans (Iru/Dawadawa) 1 tablespoon
Palm Oil 2-3 tablespoons
Onion, chopped 1 medium
Garlic, minced 2-3 cloves
Scotch Bonnet or Habanero Pepper 2-3 (adjust to taste)
Ginger, ground 1 teaspoon
Maggi/Bouillon Cubes 2 cubes
Salt & Black Pepper To taste
Water 4-5 cups

Instructions

  1. Cook the Meat: Place beef or goat meat in a large pot with chopped onions, Maggi cubes, ginger, and salt. Add water and boil until the meat is very tender (about 30-40 minutes). This creates a flavorful stock.
  2. Add Fish: Once the meat is tender, add the washed dried fish and cook for another 10-15 minutes. The fish will infuse its smoky flavor into the broth.
  3. Prepare the Base: Remove the meat and fish from the pot, leaving the stock. Return the pot to heat and add palm oil. Let it heat through for about 5 minutes (this step is optional but adds richness).
  4. Add Seasonings: Add the locust beans (iru/dawadawa) and ground crayfish to the stock. Stir well and let cook for a couple of minutes to infuse flavors.
  5. Incorporate Kuka Powder: This is the crucial step. Gradually sprinkle the kuka powder (ground baobab leaves) while stirring vigorously with a wooden spoon or whisk. Add it slowly—about 1 tablespoon at a time—to prevent lumps from forming. The soup will thicken and become sticky and smooth.
  6. Blend if Needed: If the mixture seems lumpy, you can transfer a portion to a blender and pulse a few times, then return to the pot and stir to combine.
  7. Return Proteins: Once the desired consistency is reached (the soup should be sticky and smooth), return the cooked meat and fish to the pot. Stir gently to combine.
  8. Final Cooking: Simmer on very low heat for 2-5 minutes. Taste and adjust seasonings with salt and pepper as needed. Do not overcook at this stage.
  9. Serve: Serve hot with tuwo shinkafa (fermented rice balls), tuwo masara (corn fufu), or any preferred swallow. The soup tastes even better when reheated the next day!

Tips for Success

  • Quality Meat: Use the juiciest parts of beef or goat meat (bony parts like ribs work great) for maximum flavor in the broth.
  • Whisk Technique: When adding kuka powder, keep stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Use a whisk if you have one.
  • Consistency: The soup should be thick, sticky, and creamy—similar to ogbono soup but with a darker green color.
  • Taste Notes: Miyan Kuka has a subtle tangy, smoky, and earthy flavor profile. Don't judge by its appearance; the taste is wonderful!
  • Storage: Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat gently over low heat.
