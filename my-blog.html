My Web Development Learning Journey

June 28, 2026

My introduction to the world of web development began back in 2014 during my National Diploma (ND) days at the Federal Polytechnic Mubi. It was there, in an "Intro to Web" course, that I first discovered the foundational syntax of the web and sparked a curiosity for building applications.

As my academic path progressed to Modibbo Adama University of Technology, Yola (MAUTECH), I sought out self-directed learning opportunities. During the summer break of my 200-level year, I picked up the Django Girls tutorial handbook to explore backend development. Unfortunately, severe challenges with mobile data access and various technical roadblocks forced me to temporarily abandon that pursuit.

However, the momentum returned during my 500-level year at MAUTECH. I enrolled in a formal Web Programming course and successfully designed and completed a web-based application as my final year project. Following my graduation in 2022, I spent the next few years (2023 to 2025) pivoting into data analytics and building robust skills as a data professional, which eventually led to my current role as a Program Analyst in the healthcare sector.

Because my new role heavily demands application development, I recognized the urgent need to revise and enhance my capacity in software engineering. This drive brought me to the May 2026 cohort of the eHA Academy UI/UX and Development Course, which is currently ongoing as I work to solidify my full-stack capabilities.

Overcoming the Edge: How I Almost Dropped Out of eHA Academy

June 25, 2026

To be completely honest, I have always known that my primary interests do not lie in visual design. Consequently, I found the initial UI/UX portion of the eHA Academy course to be incredibly challenging, and at times, frankly boring.

As the complex UI/UX assignments began to pile up, they collided heavily with my professional responsibilities at work and personal life commitments. Feeling completely overwhelmed, I reached a breaking point where I seriously considered dropping out of the academy entirely to relieve the pressure.

Everything changed one Sunday evening when my mentor, Itoro James, called me directly on my mobile. She reminded me about our scheduled check-in session and offered immediate encouragement. I was deeply impressed by her personal dedication and the overall commitment of the eHA team to our individual success. That conversation made me feel a strong sense of accountability and obligation to push forward.

Fast forward to today: I successfully pushed through, completed those daunting visual design assignments, and officially finished the UI/UX phase. We have now transitioned into the development part of the curriculum—covering HTML, CSS, and JavaScript—where I feel far more comfortable and genuinely excited to build.
