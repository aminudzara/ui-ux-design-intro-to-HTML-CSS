Mambilla Plateau

Elevate Your Senses in Nigeria's Highland Paradise.

Welcome to the Roof of Nigeria

Step into a world above the clouds. Located in Taraba State, the Mambilla Plateau is Nigeria’s best-kept secret—a sprawling expanse of rolling green hills, deep valleys, and breathtaking panoramic views. Averaging an elevation of 1,600 meters above sea level, the plateau offers a remarkably cool, temperate climate that serves as a refreshing escape from the tropical heat.

Here, nature remains untamed and magnificent. Whether you are seeking the thrill of conquering West Africa's highest peak, the tranquility of boundless tea estates, or the rich cultural heritage of the Mambilla and Fulani people, this highland haven promises an unforgettable adventure.

A Glimpse of the Plateau

Endless Green Valleys

Experience the mesmerizing "velvet grass" that carpets the undulating landscape. Deep gorges and panoramic views await around every corner.

Pristine Environments

Breathe in the crisp, unpolluted mountain air. The unique altitude creates a haven free from tropical pests, perfect for relaxation and hiking.

Rich Nomadic Culture

Encounter the pastoral beauty of cattle ranches and the traditional lifestyle of the nomadic Fulani herders who share the highlands.

Incredible Things to See

  • Chappal Waddi: Nigeria's highest peak at a towering 2,419 meters (7,936 ft).
  • Kakara Tea Estate: Marvel at lush, terraced tea farms.
  • Ndoro Waterfalls: A stunning cascade hidden within the rich forest.
  • The Donga River Source: Witness the serene origins of the river.
  • Apple & Strawberry Farms: Temperate fruits thrive here beautifully.

Must-Visit Places

  • Gembu Town: The largest town and headquarters; perfect for lodging.
  • Gashaka-Gumti National Park: Nigeria's largest national park.
  • Ngel Nyaki Forest Reserve: A vital montane forest ecosystem.
  • Mai Samari: Known for fear-inspiring, dramatic valleys.
  • Nguroji Town: A scenic settlement providing a fantastic vantage point.
